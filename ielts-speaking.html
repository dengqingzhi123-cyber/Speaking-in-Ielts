Part 1 · Interview (4–5 minutes)
Hobbies & Free Time · Jan 2025
  • 01 Do you have any hobbies? How long have you been doing them?
  • 02 Do you prefer spending your free time indoors or outdoors? Why?
  • 03 Have your hobbies changed since you were a child?
  • 04 Is it important for people to have hobbies? Why?
  • 05 Are there any hobbies you would like to take up in the future?
Part 2 · Long Turn (3–4 minutes)
Cue Cards · Jan–Mar 2025
  • 01 Describe a time when you used the internet to solve a problem. You should say: what the problem was, how you searched for help, whether the solution worked, and why you remember this experience.
  • 02 Describe a person in your life who has inspired you to be more environmentally conscious.
  • 03 Describe a local business you like visiting. What is it, where is it, why do you enjoy it?
Part 3 · Two-Way Discussion (4–5 minutes)
Society & Change · Oct–Dec 2024
  • 01 How has technology changed the way people socialise compared to 20 years ago?
  • 02 Do you think governments should impose stricter environmental regulations on businesses? Why or why not?
  • 03 Some people believe that remote work will eventually replace offices entirely. To what extent do you agree?
  • 04 What role should education play in addressing social inequality?
Theme · Technology (All Parts)
Technology Questions · 2024–2025
  • 01 How often do you use your smartphone? What do you mainly use it for?
  • 02 Describe a piece of technology that has significantly changed your life.
  • 03 Are there any negative effects of relying too heavily on technology in daily life?
Theme · Environment (All Parts)
Environment Questions · 2024–2025
  • 01 Do you try to live in an environmentally friendly way? Give examples.
  • 02 Describe a place in nature that is special to you and explain why it's important to protect it.
  • 03 Who is more responsible for protecting the environment — governments, businesses, or individuals?

Your response demonstrates good lexical range — the use of "well-structured" and "spend some time studying" shows natural collocation. You maintained a clear narrative structure throughout your answer with appropriate connectives.
⚠ Grammar to Review
One area to work on: avoid repeating the simple present for all actions ("I wake up... I have... I finish"). Try varying tenses — for example, using the present perfect: "I've been waking up at 7am since I started university." This signals greater grammatical range.
💡 Vocabulary Suggestion
To boost your Lexical Resource score, consider using more precise or idiomatic alternatives: instead of "relax," try "unwind," "decompress," or "switch off." Instead of "cook dinner," try "prepare a home-cooked meal." Small upgrades make a measurable difference.
